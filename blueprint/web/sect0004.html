4 Inner Product Spaces

This world explores inner product spaces, introducing the geometric structure of vector spaces through inner products, norms, and orthogonality. The 8 levels build up to fundamental inequalities including Cauchy-Schwarz and the triangle inequality.

4.1 Inner Product Definition

Definition 30 Real Inner Product Space
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 11

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.InnerProductSpace_real_v

An inner product space over \(\mathbb {R}\) is a vector space \(V\) over \(\mathbb {R}\) with an inner product \(\langle \cdot , \cdot \rangle : V \times V \to \mathbb {R}\) satisfying:

  1. Symmetry: \(\langle v, w \rangle = \langle w, v \rangle \)

  2. Linearity: \(\langle au + bv, w \rangle = a\langle u, w \rangle + b\langle v, w \rangle \)

  3. Positive definiteness: \(\langle v, v \rangle \geq 0\) with equality iff \(v = 0\)

Definition 31 Complex Inner Product Space
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 11

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.InnerProductSpace_v

An inner product space over \(\mathbb {C}\) is a vector space \(V\) over \(\mathbb {C}\) with an inner product \(\langle \cdot , \cdot \rangle : V \times V \to \mathbb {C}\) satisfying:

  1. Positivity: \(\langle v, v \rangle \in \mathbb {R}_{\geq 0}\) for all \(v \in V\)

  2. Definiteness: \(\langle v, v \rangle = 0\) iff \(v = 0\)

  3. Additivity in first slot: \(\langle u + v, w \rangle = \langle u, w \rangle + \langle v, w \rangle \)

  4. Homogeneity in first slot: \(\langle a \cdot v, w \rangle = a \cdot \langle v, w \rangle \)

  5. Conjugate symmetry: \(\langle v, w \rangle = \overline{\langle w, v \rangle }\)

4.2 Level 1: Norm Non-negativity

Definition 32 Norm
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 31

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.norm_v

The norm of a vector \(v\) is defined as:

\[ \| v\| = \sqrt{\langle v, v \rangle } \]
Theorem 33 Norm Non-negativity
#

Uses

  • Definition 32

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.norm_nonneg_v

For any vector \(v\) in an inner product space: \(\| v\| \geq 0\)

Proof ▶

Since \(\langle v, v \rangle \geq 0\) by the positivity axiom, and the square root of a non-negative real number is non-negative, we have \(\| v\| = \sqrt{\langle v, v \rangle } \geq 0\).

4.3 Level 2: Zero Norm Characterization

Theorem 34 Zero Norm
#

Uses

  • Definition 32
  • Definition 31

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.norm_zero_v

\(\| v\| = 0\) if and only if \(v = 0\)

Proof ▶

\(\| v\| = 0 \iff \sqrt{\langle v, v \rangle } = 0 \iff \langle v, v \rangle = 0 \iff v = 0\) by the definiteness axiom.

4.4 Level 3: Scalar Multiplication and Norm

Theorem 35 Scalar Multiplication of Norm
#

Uses

  • Definition 32

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.sca_mul

For any scalar \(a \in \mathbb {C}\) and vector \(v\):

\[ \| a \cdot v\| = |a| \cdot \| v\| \]
Proof ▶
\begin{align} \| a \cdot v\| ^2 & = \langle a \cdot v, a \cdot v \rangle \\ & = a \cdot \overline{a} \cdot \langle v, v \rangle \\ & = |a|^2 \cdot \| v\| ^2 \end{align}

Taking square roots gives the result.

4.6 Level 5: Pythagorean Theorem

Theorem 38 Pythagorean Theorem
#

Uses

  • Definition 36
  • Definition 32

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.pythagorean

If \(v\) and \(w\) are orthogonal, then:

\[ \| v + w\| ^2 = \| v\| ^2 + \| w\| ^2 \]
Proof ▶
\begin{align} \| v + w\| ^2 & = \langle v + w, v + w \rangle \\ & = \langle v, v \rangle + \langle v, w \rangle + \langle w, v \rangle + \langle w, w \rangle \\ & = \| v\| ^2 + 0 + 0 + \| w\| ^2 \end{align}

where \(\langle v, w \rangle = \langle w, v \rangle = 0\) by orthogonality.

4.7 Level 6: Orthogonal Decomposition

Theorem 39 Orthogonal Decomposition
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 36

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.ortho_decom

Given a vector \(v\) and a non-zero vector \(u\), we can decompose \(v\) as:

\[ v = \text{proj}_u(v) + (v - \text{proj}_u(v)) \]

where \(\text{proj}_u(v) = \frac{\langle v, u \rangle }{\langle u, u \rangle } \cdot u\) is orthogonal to \(v - \text{proj}_u(v)\).

4.8 Level 7: Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality

Theorem 40 Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality
#

Uses

  • Definition 32
  • Definition 31

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.Cauchy_Schwarz

For any vectors \(v\) and \(w\) in an inner product space:

\[ |\langle v, w \rangle | \leq \| v\| \cdot \| w\| \]

with equality if and only if \(v\) and \(w\) are linearly dependent.

Proof Sketch ▶

If \(w = 0\), both sides are zero. Otherwise, consider the orthogonal decomposition of \(v\) with respect to \(w\):

\[ v = \frac{\langle v, w \rangle }{\langle w, w \rangle } \cdot w + u \]

where \(u\) is orthogonal to \(w\). By the Pythagorean theorem:

\[ \| v\| ^2 = \left\| \frac{\langle v, w \rangle }{\langle w, w \rangle } \cdot w\right\| ^2 + \| u\| ^2 \geq \frac{|\langle v, w \rangle |^2}{\| w\| ^2} \]

Rearranging gives the inequality.

4.9 Level 8: Triangle Inequality

Theorem 41 Triangle Inequality
#

Uses

  • Theorem 40
  • Definition 32

Lean declarations

  • LinearAlgebraGame.triangle_v

For any vectors \(v\) and \(w\) in an inner product space:

\[ \| v + w\| \leq \| v\| + \| w\| \]
Proof Sketch ▶
\begin{align} \| v + w\| ^2 & = \langle v + w, v + w \rangle \\ & = \| v\| ^2 + 2\text{Re}(\langle v, w \rangle ) + \| w\| ^2 \\ & \leq \| v\| ^2 + 2|\langle v, w \rangle | + \| w\| ^2 \\ & \leq \| v\| ^2 + 2\| v\| \| w\| + \| w\| ^2 \quad \text{(by Cauchy-Schwarz)} \\ & = (\| v\| + \| w\| )^2 \end{align}

Taking square roots gives the result.

Lemma 43 Conjugation Properties
#

Uses

  • Definition 31

Complex conjugation satisfies:

  • Injectivity: \(\overline{a} = \overline{b} \iff a = b\)

  • Additivity: \(\overline{a + b} = \overline{a} + \overline{b}\)

  • Multiplicativity: \(\overline{a \cdot b} = \overline{a} \cdot \overline{b}\)

  • Zero: \(\overline{0} = 0\)

Lemma 44 Inner Product Linearity Properties
#

Uses

  • Definition 31

The inner product satisfies:

  • Right additivity: \(\langle v, w_1 + w_2 \rangle = \langle v, w_1 \rangle + \langle v, w_2 \rangle \)

  • Left zero: \(\langle 0, w \rangle = 0\)

  • Right zero: \(\langle v, 0 \rangle = 0\)

  • Right homogeneity: \(\langle v, a \cdot w \rangle = \overline{a} \cdot \langle v, w \rangle \)

  • Left negation: \(\langle -v, w \rangle = -\langle v, w \rangle \)

4.11 Summary

This world establishes the fundamental theory of inner product spaces:

  • The formal definition of inner products with their five axioms

  • The concept of norm and its properties

  • Orthogonality and the Pythagorean theorem

  • The fundamental inequalities: Cauchy-Schwarz and triangle inequality

  • Supporting properties of complex conjugation and inner product operations

These concepts provide the geometric structure needed for understanding distances, angles, and orthogonal projections in vector spaces.
